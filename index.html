Urban Data Scientist & Architect · NYU

I am an M.S. candidate in Urban Data Science at New York University, bridging the gap between the physical built environment and data-driven insights.

With the perspective of an architect, I deconstruct and restructure complex urban systems quantitatively to design human-centric environments. With the perspective of a logistics planner, I focus on optimizing the movement of people and goods to generate efficient, sustainable flows across cities. Ultimately, I leverage spatial computing and machine learning to build smarter, more resilient urban futures.
