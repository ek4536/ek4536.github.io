About

Background

I started as a licensed architect in Seoul, designing large-scale commercial and logistics projects at Gansam Architects & Partners. That foundation gave me a deep intuition for the built environment — but I wanted to work at a larger scale, with data.

At Coupang, I applied geospatial analysis to optimize a network of 400+ last-mile delivery nodes across South Korea, scaling service coverage by 200% and generating $75K in annual cost savings. Now at NYU, I'm combining machine learning, GIS, and urban theory to tackle city-scale challenges.

I'm drawn to questions where space, people, and data intersect: housing conversion, energy equity, logistics infrastructure, and urban heat.
